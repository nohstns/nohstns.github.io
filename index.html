Nafal Ossandón Hostens

About Me

Hi! I'm a fourth-year PhD Candidate at the department of Germanic Studies at the University of Texas at Austin, currently working under the supervision of professor Nick Henry in the SLAB Lab. My research centers on the role of attention and awareness in the acquisition of syntax by beginner learners of Germanic languages. My dissertation project is partially funded by an NSF Doctoral Dissertation Improvement grant (2024) and a Duolingo Dissertation grant (2023).

I have an MA in Computational Psycholinguistics from the University of Antwerp, where I worked under the supervision of professor Sarah Bernolet and professor Orphée De Clercq, from Ghent University, on the automatic assessment of the syntactic complexity of texts written by learners of Dutch as a second language. Before that, I studied German, English and Spanish Linguistics and Literature at Ghent University.

My favorite acronyms are SLA – Second Language Acquisition, and NLP – Natural Language Processing. I'm interested in the psycholinguistics of SLA, especially Input Processing and the mechanisms driving comprehension and sentence processing, both in the case of proficient, balanced bilinguals and beginner learners. I am also very interested in Computer-Assisted Language Learning, more specifically AI for accessible language education and proficiency assessment. In addition to its pedagogical applications, I am deeply interested in the cognitive insights we can gain from analyzing computational language models and the generalizations that we can and cannot draw from them.

As a language instructor, I center my classes around communication and encourage self-compassion throughout the learning process, and I integrate authentic materials and pop culture as much as possible in my classes.

I am also a Research Assistant in the UT Neurolinguistics Lab under the supervision of professor Fernando Llanos, and in professor Zenzi Griffin's Cognition and Communication Lab.

I have more hobbies than time allows, but among them I count running & dance fitness, language learning, (trying to) longboard, reading, watercolor painting, gaming and improving my coding skills. I also spend a significant amount of time with my cat Luke and my ferret Chewie.
